Hello my name is Jedidiah Sun, welcome to my website.

I am currently a third year Biological Science major attending the University of California, Riverside (UCR). I am on track to graduate this summer and hope to do so indefinitely. I would like to use my degree to teach in a public institution with a focus on STEM related material. Specifically I would like to teach biology to students in high school as I believe that life is a wonderful thing to explore as it not only tells us about the way the world works around us, but also how and where we as individuals fit in this vast world as well. My plan in life is to introduce and inspire our youth to develop a passion and interest in the STEM field and help do my part in furthering a new and brighter future in the scientific community starting with those in highschool.

Much of my experience in education has been targeted toward the medical aspect of biology as human life is what interests me. I also believe that we have a duty and responsibility to understand as much as possible the capabilities and inner workings of the bodies that we use every moment of every day. I have volunteered at the Kaiser Permanente Medical Hospital, Baldwin Park for a year and am certified in California for basic life support and as a Basic Emergency Medical Technician (EMT-B). I have completed the Chemisty (Chem001), Biology (Biol005), Biological Physics (Phys2) and Organic Chemisty (Chem008) series courses at UCR. I am currently taking my Biochemisty (BCH100) and upper division biology courses which include introductory neuroscience (CBNS106) and introductory genetics (Biol102). I particularly enjoy taking the neuroscience course as I was allowed the opprotunity to disect and examine the inner workings of a sheeps brain. Beyond Biology courses, I have also taken some introductory computer science courses as I believe that a pursuit for the understanding of technology is neccessary in order to gain a greater outlook into the various interactions of life in our world. Technology allows us to exceed our physical limitations by compensating for our flaws and thereby increasing the effienciecy of our pursuit of knowledge.

In addition to education I am also an active member of Asian American Christian Fellowship (AACF), an on campus christian organization that seeks to create a loving and open community of people here at UCR. I am serving in the small group ministry, where we engage in discussion over various life topics and seek to further the love God has for us in our own lives.

Post-Graduation, I would like to take my fourth year to invest in training and experience in my pursuit of skills and strategies to educatate and engage others in a learning environment. I plan to obtain my teaching credential at a California State college and take the California Basic Educational Skills Test in order to work as a substitute teacher congruently with my studies.
